Methodology

①
Contract data — Tango API (FPDS/USASpending)

IT services contracts from govcon.dev via the tango-python client. Filtered to NAICS 541511 (Custom Computer Programming) and 541512 (Computer Systems Design), FY2020–present.

Key fields collected per contract: obligation amount, award date, NAICS code, evaluation methodology (tradeoff_process), contract type (competition.contract_type), set-aside code, recipient UEI + name, and awarding office/department.

Source: fetch.py

▼
②
Vendor data — SAM.gov monthly bulk extract

The SAM.gov public entity extract (~1–3 GB) is downloaded once and cached. Matched on UEI (Unique Entity ID) to look up: entity incorporation date (ENTITY_START_DATE), SAM registration date, city/state, SBA small-business certifications (SBA_BUSINESS_TYPES), and employee count (NUMBER_OF_EMPLOYEES) where reported.

Source: enrich_sam.py

▼
③
Derived variables
  • Evaluation method — tradeoff_process.code: LPTA Lowest Price Technically Acceptable · TO Best-Value Tradeoff · O Other (incl. Brooks Act QBS) · null = not reported in FPDS
  • Contract structure — competition.contract_type.code: J = Firm Fixed Price (deliverable) · Y = Time & Materials · Z = Labor Hours (T&M and Labor Hours are treated as staff augmentation)
  • Small business flag — set-aside code in {SBA, 8A, 8AN, SDVOSBC, WOSB, HZC, …} or non-empty SBA_BUSINESS_TYPES from SAM (SAM-enriched charts only)
  • Vendor age — years between ENTITY_START_DATE and award date (SAM enriched)
  • New entrant — award date within 12 months of sam_registration_date (SAM enriched)
  • Market concentration — share of contracts captured by the top 10 vendors by UEI, within each eval method

Source: analyze.py

▼
④
Caveats
  • FPDS tradeoff_process is contractor-reported and often blank (~40–60% of awards). Charts that use this field show only contracts where a method was reported.
  • Fiscal year runs October–September. FY2025 data is partial until September 2025.
  • SAM employee counts are self-reported and not always current; some entities omit the field entirely.
  • This analysis covers IT services only (NAICS 541511 + 541512). Results may not generalize to other procurement categories.
